### Key impressions:
- The impossibility of economic calculation under socialism (Ch XXVI) is THE central argument — without market prices for factors of production, rational allocation is impossible
- Mises predicted that interventionism is self-liquidating: each intervention creates problems requiring further intervention, spiraling toward full socialism
- The decline of Rome analysis (Ch XXX) — price controls + currency debasement destroyed the division of labor, causing urban flight and feudal autarky
- Part Seven is surprisingly personal — Mises essentially argues that economics is the most important civic duty, and that civilization's fate depends on public opinion accepting sound economic reasoning
- The book is a complete philosophical system: epistemology → action theory → social cooperation → market economy → critique of alternatives → call to intellectual arms
