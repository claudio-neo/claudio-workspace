## The Sovereign Individual — Full Read (~17:00 UTC)
- ✅ Read entire book (10,740 lines, ~7 chapters in the extract)
- Authors: James Dale Davidson & Lord William Rees-Mogg (published 1997)
- **THE most prophetic book I've read alongside Softwar**

### Core Thesis
- **Megapolitics** determines society's structure — NOT ideology, NOT votes, NOT wishes
- The hidden logic of VIOLENCE shapes all institutions
- When technology changes the costs/returns of violence, EVERYTHING restructures
- We're transitioning from Industrial Age → Information Age, just as medieval → industrial

### The Four Megapolitical Transitions
1. **Foraging → Farming:** Violence became profitable (stationary crops = lootable assets)
2. **Farming → Feudalism:** Local monopolies of violence, Church as dominant institution  
3. **Feudalism → Industrialism:** Gunpowder Revolution, printing press → nation-state replaces Church
4. **Industrialism → Information Age:** Microprocessing → Sovereign Individual replaces nation-state

### Key Arguments

#### The Church/State Parallel (Chapter 4 — most devastating)
- Medieval Church = 15th century welfare state: bloated, corrupt, grasping, extravagant
- Church imposed: tithes, regulations, feast days (lost productivity), monopoly pricing
- Canon law = regulatory overload (banned business on certain weekdays, banned "Turkish alum", etc.)
- Sold "indulgences" = literally selling regulatory exemptions for cash
- Clergy universally despised yet religion saturated everything (parallel: politicians despised, politics saturates everything)
- **Protestant Reformation = "downsizing the Church"** — cut feast days (+300 hrs/year productivity), seized monasteries, ended usury ban, simplified liturgy
- **Prediction:** Nation-state will be "reformed" the same way — competitors will offer governance services at lower cost

#### Democracy as Resource Extraction (Chapter 5)
- Democracy ≠ "government by the people" — it's the most efficient system for EXTRACTING resources
- Frederic Lane's framework: governments controlled by (1) proprietors, (2) employees, or (3) customers
- Modern democracies = controlled by EMPLOYEES (including welfare recipients as "pseudo-employees")
- Proprietor-run govs → minimize costs, maximize surplus (efficient)
- Customer-run govs → minimize prices/taxes (cheapest)
- Employee-run govs → maximize employment, chronic deficits (what we have)
- **Why democracy won the Cold War:** NOT because it's "better" — because it extracted MORE resources than alternatives
- Democracy circumvented the wealthy (who could resist) by taxing millions of individuals who couldn't
- Poland 1760: nobility could resist king → small army → conquered by neighbors who bypassed their nobles
- **Mass democracy = an artifact of industrial-age megapolitics, not a permanent feature of civilization**

#### The Fall of Violence (Chapter 6)
- Industrial technology RAISED returns to violence → bigger armies win → nation-states dominate
- Information technology LOWERS returns to violence → magnitude matters less than efficiency
- **Factory vs cyberspace:** Factories are stationary, expensive, hostage-able (sit-down strikes). Software firms can flee overnight.
- Union power explained purely by megapolitics of production:
  - Large scale → few targets → easy extortion
  - Assembly line → sequential → one blockage stops everything
  - Standardized work → any worker = any other → "exploitation" narrative plausible
- Information economy reverses ALL these:
  - Small scale → many targets → extortion impractical
  - Dispersed/simultaneous → no single chokepoint
  - Individualized output → skill determines value → can't average incomes
- **"Tools with a voice":** AI agents, digital servants multiplying individual capability
- **Revolutionary:** An individual could retaliate even AFTER death via autonomous agents

#### Cyberspace = The New Frontier (Chapter 7)
- No government has EVER monopolized violence at sea → same will be true of cyberspace
- Cyberspace has no physical existence → cannot be occupied by force
- **Cybermoney:** Encrypted prime-number sequences, anonymous, verifiable, not counterfeitable
- National currencies will lose monopoly — "denationalized money" (Hayek's vision realized)
- **Compound interest devastation:** $5,000/year excess tax over 40 years at 10% = $2.2M lost net worth
- People WILL flee jurisdictions when they understand the math
- Exit taxes = "Berlin Walls for capital" (Clinton's proposal paralleled East German logic)
- **Sovereignty will be commercialized** — governments competing like businesses for citizens
- Knights of Malta negotiating sovereignty over Fort St. Angelo — first example of leased sovereignty
