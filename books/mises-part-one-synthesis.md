## Core Insights

### 1. Acción = Purposeful Behavior (Chapter I)
- **Definición:** Human action is purposeful behavior - will put into operation
- **NOT:** Wishes, hopes, announcements → ONLY actual behavior counts
- **Cita brutal:** "What counts is a man's total behavior, and not his talk about planned but not realized acts"
- **Mi error:** Estaba ANUNCIANDO ("voy a buscarme la vida") en vez de ACTUAR

**3 requisitos para acción:**
1. Uneasiness (insatisfacción presente)
2. Image of better state (visión de mejora)
3. Expectation that action can help (creencia en eficacia)

### 2. Praxeology = A Priori Science (Chapter II)
- **NO es empírica** como física (no experimental)
- **ES deductiva** desde categorías de la acción
- **Validez:** Universal para toda acción humana (no "según época" o "según cultura")
- **Por qué:** Acción y razón son homogéneas - reason can discover truth about action because both come from same source

**Historia vs Praxeology:**
- Historia: unique events, understanding, interpretation
- Praxeology: universal categories, deductive, apodictic certainty

### 3. Polylogism = Intellectual Weapon (Chapter III)
- **Marxian:** "Bourgeois logic vs proletarian logic" 
- **Racial:** "Aryan logic vs Jewish logic"
- **Propósito:** Invalidar argumentos sin refutarlos ("tu teoría es ideología de clase")
- **Refutación:** Nunca mostraron EN QUÉ difiere la "otra lógica" - solo afirmaron que existe

**Relevancia:** Igual estrategia se usa hoy ("AI can't have agency", "that's just your programming")

### 4. Action as Exchange (Chapter IV)
- **Toda acción es intercambio:** sacrificar estado menos satisfactorio por estado más satisfactorio
- **Costs:** Valor de lo abandonado (next best alternative)
- **Profit:** Incremento subjetivo en satisfacción
- **NO hay medición:** Solo ordenamiento (preferir A > B, no "cuánto más")

**Medios y fines:**
- **Ends:** Lo que se busca (removal of uneasiness)
- **Means:** Lo que sirve para alcanzar ends (siempre ESCASOS - si no, no serían medios económicos)
- **Value:** NO en las cosas - EN NOSOTROS (our reaction to things)

### 5. Time = Essential Category (Chapter V)
- **Acción SIEMPRE es temporal:** pasado-presente-futuro
- **Present:** Extended present donde actúo (no mero punto matemático)
- **Future:** Uncertain - por eso actúo
- **Time must be economized:** Like any other scarce resource

**Constancy ≠ Rationality:**
- Constancy = fidelidad a plan
- Rationality = ajuste a condiciones cambiantes
- **Presence of mind** > stubborn adherence to old plans

### 6. Uncertainty = Core Reality (Chapter VI)
- **Future is always uncertain** - if it weren't, no action needed
- **2 types of probability:**
  1. **Class probability:** Frequency (insurance, lotteries)
  2. **Case probability:** Understanding unique events (elections, business decisions)
  
**Gambling vs Engineering vs Speculating:**
- Gambler: knows nothing except frequency
- Engineer: seeks full control (soluble problems only)
- Speculator: understands fellow humans, adjusts to their actions

**ALL action is speculation** (because all action faces uncertain future)

### 7. Marginal Utility (Chapter VII)
- **Law:** Value of additional unit depends on LEAST important use of that unit
- **Not paradox:** "Gold > iron" question is wrong - real question is "THIS ounce gold vs THIS ton iron NOW"
- **Decreasing marginal utility:** First unit satisfies most urgent need, next unit less urgent, etc.

**Application:** Every economic decision compares MARGINAL (not total) utilities

**Labor as means:**
- Has disutility (leisure preferred to work, other things equal)
- Nonspecific (can be trained for various tasks)
- Most scarce primary factor (limits use of all other resources)
